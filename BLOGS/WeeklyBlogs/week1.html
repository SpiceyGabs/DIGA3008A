I chose to read the library of Babel because of the similarity to the story in the Bible called the tower of Babel, in which the people of the time we’re trying to build a building or skyscraper tall enough to reach the heavens and to see God. To stop them, God created a confusion of language, this meant that everyone spoke different languages and couldn’t understand each other and could not continue to build anything

The library of Babel by Jorge Luis Borges, was an interesting read. The reading speaks about a universe envisioned as a library that has all possible books. The library is made up of hexagonal galleries that are linked by airshafts and low railings. it then goes on to describe a very complicated system of organisation with each book having more than 400 pages each and it’s composition. It is similar to the story of the tower of babel in that at the beginning people were happy to have a plethora of information at their fingertips and a clear idea of the sharing of information, however as time went on that happiness soon turned into depression once they realised that the precious books are inaccessible. Someone wanted to create canonical books by chance or others wanted to eliminate useless works. And very much like how the people were building in vain in the Bible these people searched for “the man of the book” in vain.

What does this have to do with my experience of the web? Well, I think most pursuits are in vain, most searches are done for fun, people do not really understand the gravitas of the situation of being able to have all and any knowledge at our fingertips. Much like what we discussed in class, human behaviour is predictable and doesn’t change especially when it comes to content.
